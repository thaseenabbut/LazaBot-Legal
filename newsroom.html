LazaBot Is Going for Clean, Interactive Setup Panels with Better Visuals

As development resumes, LazaBot is doubling down on one clear design direction — every setup flow should feel polished, intentional, and easy to navigate. Rather than plain text responses, each system now communicates through rich embeds with clear status indicators, action buttons, and structured field layouts.

The screenshots below show this in action across the Starboard and Giveaway systems — two of the first features to receive the updated panel treatment.

  • Structured field layouts — every panel surfaces the right info at the right time, no guesswork for the admin.
  • Clear action buttons — contextual buttons like Enable, Disable, Delete Data, and Launch Giveaway replace manual commands.
  • Status-aware embeds — each panel reflects the current system state with colour-coded accents and live field values.
  • Safety confirmations — destructive actions like data deletion require an explicit confirmation step before executing.

LU47.2 February 25, 2026

FlowControl Discontinued & Stability Improvements

To ensure peak performance during the development hiatus, LU47.2 ships the discontinuation of the underutilized FlowControl feature alongside a batch of targeted bug fixes. The update streamlines the bot, reducing potential instability while keeping all core functionality intact.

  • FlowControl removed — introduced in LU29, discontinued due to low adoption and maintenance overhead.
  • /warn command crash resolved in high-volume servers.
  • AFK status auto-removal now works reliably across all edge cases.
  • Webhook logging enhanced for better delivery consistency.
  • General performance optimizations for Event-Based AutoRoles and the Advanced Suggestion System.
